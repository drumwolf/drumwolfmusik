As I discussed in my last post, when alternative rock found mainstream success in the early '90s,
it did not translate into rock stardom for the vast majority of bands in the
underground music scene at the time.
However, while the bands mentioned in that last post deserved better than they got,
at least they still retained *some* level of recognition, to the point where they have able to
retain steady cult followings to this day.

Here, we're going to dig deeper into the cracks, as most of the bands here don't get
sufficient recognition even among grunge/punk/alt-rock historians and music geeks.
If Part 1's bands were overlooked, these six are practically invisible --
but they shaped the sound of '90s alternative rock just as much.

---

### Bullet LaVolta

This Boston quintet weren't grunge, but a distinctive combination of punk rock, heavy metal
and post-hardcore influenced by the likes of Fugazi, Husker Du and Naked Raygun.  Four of their 
five members were college disk jockeys, and they got their start playing college shows in Boston (usually at either MIT or Harvard).
